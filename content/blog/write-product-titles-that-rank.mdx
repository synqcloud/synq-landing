A product title is the single most important text for both shoppers and search engines. For trading cards, a clear, specific title helps the right card show up when someone searches for it. In Synq you set the format once with a [title template](/help/product-templates), and every card follows it.

## What should a card title contain?

Include the details buyers actually search for:

- **Card name**: always.
- **Set name**: many cards repeat across sets, so this pins down the exact card.
- **Collector number**: pins down the exact card.
- **Game and printing** where helpful, for example Foil or 1st Edition.

A title like `Gateway Plaza - Guilds of Ravnica - 247` tells a shopper and a search engine exactly which card this is.

## Why does this help ranking?

- **Matches what people search**: buyers search by card plus set, so titles that contain both rank for those searches.
- **Avoids duplicates**: a name-only title looks identical across sets, which is weak for search. Adding set and number makes each title unique.
- **Improves on-site search**: your own store search and collection filters work better with descriptive titles.

## How do I apply this to every card?

Set a [title template](/help/product-templates) once, using macros such as `{name}`, `{set_name}` and `{collector_number}`. Synq fills them for every product, so a thousand cards all follow the same clean format with no manual work. You can also use different templates for singles and sealed.

Titles bring people in; descriptions keep your pages from looking thin or duplicated. See [avoid thin or duplicate descriptions](/blog/avoid-thin-or-duplicate-descriptions).
